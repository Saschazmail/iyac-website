The In Your Corner Score — Our Proprietary Rating System

Not all attorneys are created equal. After years of research, client interviews, and analysis of thousands of Nevada personal injury cases, our team developed the In Your Corner Score — a proprietary algorithm designed to cut through marketing budgets and surface the attorneys who actually deliver results for their clients.

Our scoring model evaluates each firm across dozens of weighted factors — from courtroom win rates and settlement averages to client communication scores and peer reputation. Among the key data sources we incorporate are Avvo (the legal industry's most comprehensive attorney rating platform), Martindale-Hubbell (the gold standard of peer review since 1868), and Super Lawyers (a rigorous peer-nomination process that selects less than 5% of attorneys in each state).

But our algorithm goes further. We factor in case specialization depth, trial willingness, staff-to-case ratios, response time data, and direct client feedback — because the attorney who wins the most awards isn't always the one who will fight hardest for your specific situation.

The result is a 1–5 star score that reflects not just reputation, but real-world performance. No attorney can pay to improve their IYC Score. It is earned — not bought.

The Legal Process

What Happens After You Hire an Attorney

Most people have never needed a PI attorney. Here's what to expect — no legal jargon.

1
Free Consultation

Every PI attorney on our list offers a free consultation. You meet, explain what happened, and they tell you if you have a case. No obligation, no payment at this stage — ever.

2
Signing the Retainer

If you proceed, you sign a contingency fee agreement. Your attorney only gets paid if you win — typically 33% of the settlement. You pay zero upfront.

3
Investigation & Medical Treatment

Your attorney gathers evidence, police reports, and witness statements. Your job: follow your doctor's treatment plan. Documented medical treatment is the backbone of your case value.

4
Demand Letter & Negotiation

Once treatment is complete, your attorney sends a demand letter to the insurance company. Negotiation begins. Most cases settle without trial — typically within 6–18 months.

5
Settlement or Trial

If a fair settlement is reached you get your check (minus fees and medical liens). If not, your attorney takes it to trial. The attorneys on our list are not afraid of either outcome.

Questions to Ask

Before You Sign Anything

Ask every attorney these questions before you commit to representation.

Not all PI cases are the same. A car accident involving a tourist is different from a commercial truck accident on I-15. Ask specifically about their experience with your type of accident, injury severity, and insurance situation.
At larger firms, senior partners often sign cases but hand them to junior associates or paralegals. Ask directly who will be working your case day-to-day — this is your right as a client.
Standard contingency is 33% settled, often 40% at trial. But there are also case costs — filing fees, experts, records. Ask whether those come off the top before or after the attorney's percentage. The difference can be thousands of dollars.
A good attorney gives you a realistic range with reasoning — policy limits, liability percentage, injury severity. If they give you an unrealistically high number in the consultation, that's a red flag.
Communication is the #1 complaint clients have about PI attorneys. Get specifics: response time, who to contact, how often you'll hear from them. Getting this in writing as part of your retainer is completely reasonable.
Insurance companies know which attorneys actually go to trial. An attorney willing to go to court often gets better settlement offers because insurers know they can't just low-ball and walk away.
You always have the right to switch. Your original attorney may have a lien for work performed. Ask about this upfront — and know that In Your Corner can help you navigate a switch if it ever comes to that. We're with you throughout.
